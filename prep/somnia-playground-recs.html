1. Diagnosis — why the current Somnia section feels lacking

What's there now

  • 4 screenshots cycled with a single "Next" button (battle, Veilith, map, Sable)
  • One short paragraph per image — captions are fine but disposable
  • No way to see a specific feature on demand — visitors must click through linearly
  • No representation of: Bond mechanic, Training/Tutorial mode, Dream Surge, NPC dialogue, the broader world (atmospheric/cinematic shots), or the breadth of element types

Why this underwhelms

  • Single board image ≠ "match-3 RPG." A casual visitor sees one screenshot of tiles and doesn't grasp the systems on top: bonding, timed challenges, surges, multi-step combat.
  • The art is a feature. The game's painterly forest/stone backgrounds and watercolor character art are a clear style differentiator. None of that voice comes through in the current 4-shot carousel.
  • The narrative voice is a feature. Thea's dialogue ("I thought Reveries were just vibes"), the atmospheric cards ("The trees thin...") show the game has real writing personality. Right now the page just describes the genre.
  • No depth gradient. Visitors who want a quick "what is this?" get the same flow as visitors who want to dig in. There's no tl;dr and no deeper layer.

Why segmented over carousel

  • Carousels hide content behind clicks — bad for scannability
  • A page about a puzzle/RPG game should feel rich and dense, not slideshow-thin
  • Each section can be deep-linked or shared individually later
  • Lets you keep growing — adding a section is additive, not a rework

Where small carousels still help

  • The Combat section can use a 2-shot toggle (puzzle goals ↔ battle) if you want to call out the "objective view vs. live battle" framing
  • The Cast & story section: tap a character to swap dialogue line — like a hover/tap interaction, not a forced timer

5. Copy & microcopy

Caption drafts — short, concrete, written like marketing copy not feature lists.

Hero / atmosphere

"Step into a dreamscape — past the boundary of the outer reach."

Combat — the puzzle layer

Combat is a turn-based puzzle. Match elemental tiles to fuel each Reverie's skills — fire, water, air, light, dusk — and chain them into bigger plays. Hit the round's objectives in the moves you have, or change up your team.

The Bond

The bond meter charges as you fight. Fill it, and you can attempt a Dream Bond with the enemy you're battling — turning a fight into a recruitment. The signature mechanic of Somnia, and the one that hooks runs together.

Training mode

Drop into pure puzzle challenges to learn the systems — timed matches, element goals, free retries. Useful for new players and a sandbox for tuning encounters.

Cast & story

Reveries are characters, not just party slots. Drifters like Thea narrate the in-between — the moments of arriving in this world, taking notes, asking the things you'd ask. Dialogue, story choices, and rest beats sit between every encounter.

The world

Areas branch into nodes — combat, dialogue, rest, hidden rooms. Each run is a path through the dreamscape that you choose, then live with.

Avoid the words "feature-rich," "engaging," "immersive." Use the game's own vocabulary: Reverie, Bond, Dream Bond, Drifter, the outer reach, Reverie type names. The vocabulary itself does most of the worldbuilding.

7. Implementation phases

Phase 1 — content swap (smallest change)

  1. Copy + downsize the 6 new screenshots into src/client/img/ with the names above
  2. Extend the existing arrays in SomniaComponent.jsx to include all new images and captions
  3. Ship — same UI, more content. Confirms the captions and image weights work.

Effort: ~30 min

Phase 2 — restructure layout

  1. Replace the single-image-with-Next-button layout with a vertical sectioned layout in SomniaComponent.jsx
  2. Add a SomniaSection sub-component (image[s] + heading + caption block)
  3. Add styles in index.css under a .somnia-* namespace; reuse the playground typography scale (font sizes 22 / 14 / 12)
  4. Make character grid 3-up on desktop, stacked on mobile (the existing playground page already maxes at 960px — match that)

Effort: ~2–3 hours

Phase 3 — atmosphere & polish

  • Hero strip uses the forest atmosphere image as a background with a dark gradient and an italic line of copy on top — gives the section a real "land here" moment
  • Each section gets a subtle accent stripe in its corresponding element color (e.g. Bond = the bond-blue, Combat = neutral, Cast = warm)
  • Consider a "Reverie" detail card on hover/tap that pulls forward role/passive — mirrors the in-game UI

Effort: ~2 hours, optional

8. Stretch ideas (later, not now)

  • Tile-match preview animation. A small CSS/JS demo of element tiles matching and clearing, embedded above the Combat section. Sells the loop better than a static image ever can.
  • Reverie lineup widget. Pull the team art into a horizontal row of trading-card-style portraits the way the in-game UI does. Hover/tap reveals role + element.
  • Inline element legend. Six small tiles labeled fire/water/air/light/dusk/wind so visitors can read the icons in subsequent screenshots. Tiny cognitive aid that pays off across the whole section.
  • "Patch notes" feed. Since Somnia is being actively built, a 3-line "what's new this month" block under the section signals it's a live project, not a finished portfolio entry.
  • TestFlight / wait-list link. If/when there's something to opt into, the Somnia section is the natural CTA point.
